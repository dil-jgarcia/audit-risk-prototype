Note 3: Revenue Recognition

Revenue is recognized when control of the promised goods or services is transferred to the customer, in an amount that reflects the consideration the Company expects to be entitled to in exchange for those goods or services. The Company recognized $12.4 million in product revenue during the final week of the quarter, representing approximately 18% of total quarterly revenue.2 This concentration was primarily driven by large enterprise deals with extended sales cycles that closed in the final days of the period.


Management has determined that this revenue concentration pattern, while elevated compared to historical norms of 8-10%, reflects legitimate business activity and the timing of customer purchase decisions.3 The Company has reviewed the terms of these arrangements and confirmed that all revenue recognition criteria under ASC 606 have been met.
